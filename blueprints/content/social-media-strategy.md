# Social Media Strategy

A comprehensive framework for building and executing a social media strategy that grows audience engagement, strengthens brand presence, and drives measurable business outcomes. This blueprint covers auditing your current state, setting goals, planning content, and establishing a measurement framework to continuously improve performance.

## Objective

Develop a complete social media strategy document with platform-specific plans, content pillars, a posting schedule, engagement guidelines, and a measurement framework that connects social activity to business goals.

## Steps

### 1. Audit Current Social Media Presence
Review all existing social media accounts and document their current status: follower counts, posting frequency, engagement rates, top-performing content, and audience demographics. Identify what is working well and what is underperforming. Check for brand consistency across platforms in terms of bios, profile images, and tone of voice. Note any abandoned or unofficial accounts that need to be claimed or closed.

### 2. Define Social Media Goals
Set 3-5 specific goals that tie directly to business objectives. Common goals include increasing brand awareness (measured by reach and impressions), driving website traffic (measured by click-throughs), generating leads (measured by form fills or sign-ups), building community (measured by engagement rate), and supporting customer retention (measured by response time and sentiment). Each goal should have a numeric target and a timeframe.

### 3. Identify and Profile the Target Audience
Define who you want to reach on social media by building detailed audience profiles. Include demographics, interests, online behavior patterns, preferred platforms, and the type of content they engage with most. Use analytics from your existing accounts and customer data to validate assumptions. Segment your audience if you serve multiple customer types and note how their social media habits differ.

### 4. Choose and Prioritize Platforms
Select the 2-4 platforms where your target audience is most active and where your content format strengths align. For each platform, define its role in your strategy: is it for brand awareness, community building, lead generation, or customer support? Avoid spreading resources too thin across every platform. It is better to excel on three platforms than to be mediocre on six.

### 5. Define Content Pillars
Establish 4-6 content pillars that represent the recurring themes of your social media content. Each pillar should connect to your brand values and audience interests. Examples include educational content, behind-the-scenes, customer stories, industry insights, product highlights, and community engagement. For each pillar, define the content formats that work best (video, carousel, text post, stories) and the percentage of total content it should represent.

### 6. Create a Posting Schedule
Build a weekly posting calendar that specifies what to post, on which platform, and at what time. Use platform analytics to identify when your audience is most active. Define a sustainable posting frequency for each platform that your team can maintain consistently. Include a mix of content pillars throughout the week and leave room for timely, reactive content. Batch content creation to improve efficiency.

### 7. Develop an Engagement Strategy
Define how your team will interact with the audience beyond just publishing content. Set response time targets for comments and direct messages. Outline guidelines for tone and voice in replies. Plan proactive engagement activities: commenting on relevant industry conversations, participating in trending topics, collaborating with complementary brands, and amplifying user-generated content. Build community by making followers feel heard and valued.

### 8. Build a Measurement Framework
Create a reporting dashboard that tracks performance against the goals set in Step 2. Define which metrics to monitor weekly (engagement rate, reach, follower growth) versus monthly (traffic driven, leads generated, sentiment trends). Establish a monthly review process where the team analyzes what worked, what did not, and what to test next. Use insights from measurement to continuously refine content pillars, posting times, and platform priorities.

## Expected Output

A social media strategy document containing an audit summary, defined goals with targets, audience profiles, platform-specific plans, content pillar definitions, a weekly posting calendar template, engagement guidelines, and a measurement dashboard with reporting cadence.

## Recommended Agents

- **CMO** — Sets the strategic direction and ensures social media goals align with the broader marketing strategy.
- **Content Strategist** — Defines content pillars, creates the editorial calendar, and maintains quality and consistency across platforms.
- **Brand Strategist** — Ensures all social media content and interactions are on-brand in tone, visuals, and messaging.
- **Data Analyst** — Builds the measurement framework, tracks performance metrics, and delivers insights for optimization.
