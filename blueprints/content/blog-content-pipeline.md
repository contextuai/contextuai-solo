# Blog Content Pipeline

A structured end-to-end pipeline for producing high-quality blog content that drives organic traffic and supports business goals. This blueprint covers every stage from ideation through publication, ensuring each piece of content is strategically aligned, well-researched, properly optimized, and published on a consistent schedule.

## Objective

Establish a repeatable content production pipeline that takes a topic from initial idea to published blog post, with built-in quality gates for SEO optimization, editorial standards, and strategic alignment.

## Steps

### 1. Topic Ideation
Generate a list of 10-20 candidate topics by mining multiple sources: customer questions from support tickets, keyword gap analysis, competitor content audits, industry news, sales team feedback, and internal subject matter expertise. For each topic, note the target audience, the search intent it addresses (informational, navigational, commercial, transactional), and how it connects to your product or service. Prioritize topics that sit at the intersection of audience interest and business relevance.

### 2. Keyword Research
For each prioritized topic, identify a primary keyword and 3-5 secondary keywords using SEO tools and search analysis. Evaluate search volume, keyword difficulty, and current SERP competition. Look for long-tail variations that offer realistic ranking opportunities. Analyze the top-ranking pages for your primary keyword to understand what content format, depth, and angle Google is rewarding. Document the target keyword cluster for each post.

### 3. Create the Outline
Build a detailed outline that defines the structure of the post before any drafting begins. Include the working title (incorporating the primary keyword), the H2 and H3 sections, key points to cover under each section, data or examples to include, and the intended word count. The outline should make it clear what each section needs to accomplish and how the post flows from introduction through to conclusion and call-to-action.

### 4. Write the Draft
Produce the first draft following the approved outline. Write for the target audience's knowledge level and use a tone consistent with your brand voice guidelines. Lead with the most valuable information, use short paragraphs and clear subheadings, and include relevant examples, data points, or case studies. Naturally incorporate the primary and secondary keywords without forcing them. Aim for comprehensive coverage that answers the reader's question better than any competing page.

### 5. Editorial Review
Review the draft for clarity, accuracy, grammar, brand voice consistency, and logical flow. Check that all claims are supported by evidence, all links work, and all examples are current. Verify that the post delivers on the promise of its headline and that the conclusion includes a clear next step for the reader. Provide specific, actionable feedback and iterate until the piece meets editorial standards.

### 6. SEO Optimization
Optimize the final draft for search engines without compromising readability. Ensure the primary keyword appears in the title, meta description, first paragraph, at least one H2, and the URL slug. Add internal links to 3-5 related posts and at least 2 authoritative external links. Optimize images with descriptive alt text and compressed file sizes. Write a compelling meta description under 155 characters that encourages click-through from search results.

### 7. Publish and Distribute
Schedule the post according to your content calendar. Set up social media shares, email newsletter inclusion, and any paid promotion. After publishing, submit the URL to search console for indexing. Monitor initial performance metrics (page views, time on page, bounce rate) during the first 48 hours and make any necessary adjustments to the title or meta description based on early click-through data.

## Expected Output

A published blog post with optimized SEO elements, a distribution plan across owned channels, and a performance tracking setup. Additionally, a documented pipeline template that can be reused for future content production.

## Recommended Agents

- **Content Strategist** — Leads topic ideation, defines the content calendar, and ensures each piece aligns with the overall content strategy.
- **Market Researcher** — Provides keyword research data, competitive content analysis, and audience insight to inform topic selection.
- **Brand Strategist** — Reviews drafts for brand voice consistency and ensures messaging aligns with brand positioning.
