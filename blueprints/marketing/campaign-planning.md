# Marketing Campaign Planning

A comprehensive framework for planning and executing a marketing campaign from concept to launch. This blueprint covers every stage of campaign development, ensuring teams have a clear objective, a well-defined audience, the right channels, compelling messaging, and measurable outcomes before spending a single dollar.

## Objective

Produce a complete marketing campaign plan with defined goals, target audience profiles, channel strategy, messaging framework, budget allocation, KPIs, and a detailed timeline ready for execution.

## Steps

### 1. Define the Campaign Objective
Start by articulating exactly what this campaign needs to achieve. Is it brand awareness, lead generation, product launch, customer retention, or event promotion? Set a SMART goal: specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound. For example, "Generate 500 qualified leads for the new product within 60 days of launch." A clear objective prevents scope creep and gives every subsequent decision a north star.

### 2. Identify the Target Audience
Define who you are trying to reach with precision. Build detailed audience profiles that include demographics, psychographics, pain points, buying behavior, and preferred media channels. Segment the audience if the campaign targets multiple groups, and prioritize the segment most likely to convert. Use existing customer data, CRM analytics, and market research to validate your assumptions rather than relying on guesswork.

### 3. Choose Marketing Channels
Select the channels that will most effectively reach your target audience given your budget and timeline. Evaluate paid channels (search ads, social ads, display, sponsored content), owned channels (email, blog, social media, website), and earned channels (PR, influencer partnerships, word-of-mouth). For each channel, estimate the expected reach, cost per acquisition, and how it fits into the overall customer journey from awareness to conversion.

### 4. Craft the Messaging Framework
Develop the core message, supporting messages, and calls-to-action for the campaign. The core message should communicate the primary value proposition in one sentence. Supporting messages address specific audience pain points and objections. Create message variations tailored to each channel and audience segment. Ensure tone, language, and visual direction are consistent across all touchpoints while being optimized for each platform's format.

### 5. Set the Budget
Allocate budget across channels, creative production, tools, and contingency. Use historical performance data to inform allocation: put more money where past campaigns have delivered the best ROI. Reserve 10-15% as a contingency fund for mid-campaign optimization or unexpected opportunities. Document all assumptions and create a simple cost model that connects spend to expected outcomes.

### 6. Define KPIs and Measurement Plan
Establish the key performance indicators that will determine whether the campaign succeeds. Include leading indicators (impressions, click-through rates, engagement) and lagging indicators (conversions, revenue, customer acquisition cost). Set up tracking infrastructure before launch: UTM parameters, conversion pixels, attribution models, and reporting dashboards. Define what "good" looks like for each KPI with specific numeric targets.

### 7. Create the Campaign Timeline
Build a detailed timeline working backward from the launch date. Include milestones for creative development, content production, asset review and approval, channel setup, QA testing, launch, and post-launch optimization windows. Assign owners to each milestone and build in buffer time for revisions. Schedule regular check-ins during the campaign to review performance and make adjustments.

## Expected Output

A campaign brief document containing the objective, audience profiles, channel strategy with budget allocation, messaging framework with creative direction, KPI targets with measurement plan, and a week-by-week execution timeline with assigned owners.

## Recommended Agents

- **CMO** — Sets the strategic direction, approves the messaging framework, and ensures the campaign aligns with brand positioning.
- **Content Strategist** — Develops the messaging framework, creates content briefs, and ensures consistency across channels.
- **Brand Strategist** — Ensures visual and tonal consistency, reviews creative direction, and protects brand equity.
- **Data Analyst** — Defines KPIs, sets up tracking infrastructure, and creates the measurement and reporting plan.
