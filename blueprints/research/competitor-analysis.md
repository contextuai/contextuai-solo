# Competitor Analysis

A structured framework for conducting thorough competitor analysis that goes beyond surface-level observation to deliver actionable strategic intelligence. This blueprint guides teams through identifying competitors, dissecting their offerings, comparing positioning, and translating findings into strategic advantages for your own business.

## Objective

Produce a comprehensive competitor analysis report that maps the competitive landscape, identifies each competitor's strengths and vulnerabilities, and delivers specific strategic recommendations for differentiation and market positioning.

## Steps

### 1. Identify and Classify Competitors
Build a complete list of competitors by category: direct competitors (same product, same market), indirect competitors (different product, same customer need), and emerging competitors (startups or adjacent players moving into your space). Use industry reports, search engine results for your primary keywords, customer interviews, and sales team intelligence to ensure the list is thorough. Select 4-6 competitors for deep analysis based on market relevance and threat level.

### 2. Analyze Products and Services
For each competitor, document their full product or service offering. Examine features, pricing tiers, packaging, delivery model, and user experience. Sign up for free trials or demos where possible to experience the product firsthand. Note areas where their offering is stronger than yours, where it is weaker, and where they have features you lack entirely. Pay attention to their product roadmap signals: blog announcements, job postings, and patent filings.

### 3. Compare Pricing and Positioning
Map each competitor's pricing strategy, including their entry-level price, mid-tier, and enterprise offerings. Analyze how they frame value: do they compete on price, features, service quality, or brand prestige? Document their positioning statements, taglines, and core messaging. Create a positioning map that plots competitors along two key dimensions relevant to your market (such as price vs. feature richness or simplicity vs. power).

### 4. Review Marketing and Go-to-Market Strategy
Examine how each competitor acquires and retains customers. Audit their website messaging, content marketing, social media presence, advertising spend (using tools like SimilarWeb or SpyFu), email campaigns, and PR activity. Identify which channels they invest most heavily in and where they appear to be gaining traction. Note their content themes, publishing frequency, and the topics they own in search rankings.

### 5. SWOT Analysis per Competitor
Conduct a mini-SWOT for each analyzed competitor, identifying their key strengths, weaknesses, opportunities they might exploit, and threats they face. This structured view makes it easier to spot vulnerabilities you can exploit and strengths you need to counter. Be honest about where competitors outperform you and resist the temptation to dismiss their advantages.

### 6. Identify Market Gaps and White Space
Synthesize findings from all competitors to identify needs that are poorly served or completely unaddressed in the market. Look for common customer complaints across competitors, feature requests that nobody has fulfilled, underserved customer segments, and pricing gaps. These white spaces represent your highest-potential opportunities for differentiation. Rank them by market size and your ability to credibly address them.

### 7. Develop Strategic Recommendations
Translate your analysis into 5-7 specific, actionable recommendations. Each recommendation should reference the competitive intelligence that supports it and include a clear rationale for why it will create advantage. Cover product strategy (features to build or emphasize), marketing strategy (positioning and messaging adjustments), pricing strategy (opportunities to reposition), and sales strategy (competitive battlecards and objection handling). Prioritize recommendations by impact and feasibility.

## Expected Output

A competitor analysis report containing a competitive landscape map, detailed profiles of 4-6 key competitors, a comparative feature and pricing matrix, individual competitor SWOT analyses, an identified market gaps summary, and a prioritized list of strategic recommendations with supporting evidence.

## Recommended Agents

- **Market Researcher** — Leads the research effort, gathers competitive intelligence, and ensures analysis is grounded in data.
- **Product Manager** — Evaluates competitor products, identifies feature gaps, and translates findings into product strategy.
- **CMO** — Assesses competitor marketing strategies and refines your positioning and messaging based on the analysis.
- **CEO** — Reviews the strategic recommendations and decides which competitive moves to prioritize at the company level.
