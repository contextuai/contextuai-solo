# OKR Planning Workshop

A structured workshop for setting Objectives and Key Results (OKRs) that align the entire organization around measurable goals. This blueprint walks teams through defining a clear vision, translating it into ambitious objectives, and establishing quantifiable key results that drive accountability and focus.

## Objective

Produce a complete set of company and team-level OKRs for the upcoming quarter, with clearly assigned owners, defined measurement methods, and an agreed-upon review cadence.

## Steps

### 1. Clarify Company Vision and Strategic Priorities
Begin by reviewing or articulating the company's mission and long-term vision. Identify the 2-3 strategic themes that matter most for the upcoming quarter based on where the business stands today. Consider recent performance data, market conditions, and any shifts in company direction. This step ensures that every OKR that follows is anchored to something that genuinely matters.

### 2. Set Company-Level Objectives
Draft 3-5 ambitious, qualitative objectives that describe what the organization wants to achieve this quarter. Each objective should be inspirational, time-bound, and clearly connected to the strategic priorities from Step 1. Good objectives are memorable and motivating, not vague platitudes. Test each one by asking: if we achieved this, would it meaningfully move the business forward?

### 3. Define Key Results for Each Objective
For each objective, write 2-4 key results that are specific, measurable, and time-bound. Key results should describe outcomes, not activities. Use concrete numbers wherever possible (revenue targets, percentage improvements, user counts). Set them at a stretch level where achieving 70% would still represent strong progress. For each key result, document the current baseline, the target, and exactly how it will be measured.

### 4. Cascade to Team and Individual OKRs
Work with each team to create their own OKRs that ladder up to the company objectives. Teams should have 2-3 objectives with their own key results that contribute directly to at least one company-level key result. Ensure there is clear alignment without micromanaging how teams achieve their goals. Identify any dependencies between teams and flag them for coordination.

### 5. Assign Owners and Resources
Every objective and key result needs a single accountable owner, even if execution involves multiple people. Document who owns what, what resources they need, and any budget implications. Identify potential conflicts with existing workloads and make explicit trade-off decisions about what will be deprioritized to make room for OKR work.

### 6. Establish Review Cadence and Rituals
Set up a regular check-in schedule: weekly team-level updates, bi-weekly cross-team syncs, and a monthly company-wide review. Define the format for check-ins including how progress will be reported (on-track, at-risk, off-track), what triggers an escalation, and how OKRs can be adjusted mid-quarter if circumstances change. Document these rituals so they become habits.

## Expected Output

A published OKR document containing company-level and team-level objectives with measurable key results, ownership assignments, a resource allocation summary, and a calendar of review checkpoints for the quarter.

## Recommended Agents

- **CEO** — Sets the strategic vision and approves company-level objectives to ensure alignment with long-term goals.
- **COO** — Coordinates cross-team dependencies and ensures operational capacity exists to deliver on key results.
- **Product Manager** — Translates product strategy into team-level OKRs and defines measurable outcomes for product initiatives.
- **Data Analyst** — Establishes baselines, defines measurement methodologies, and builds dashboards for tracking key results.
