# Lean Canvas Brainstorm

A structured brainstorming session using the Lean Canvas framework to rapidly validate and refine a business idea. This blueprint walks through all nine blocks of the Lean Canvas, helping teams move from a vague concept to a testable business model in a single working session.

## Objective

Produce a completed Lean Canvas for a new business idea or product concept, identifying the riskiest assumptions and outlining next steps for validation.

## Steps

### 1. Define the Problem
Identify the top 1-3 problems your target customers face. For each problem, describe who experiences it, how frequently it occurs, and what existing alternatives they currently use to work around it. Rank the problems by severity and frequency to determine which is the most urgent to solve.

### 2. Identify Customer Segments
List the specific groups of people who experience the problems you defined. Go beyond broad demographics and describe early adopters who are most likely to pay for a solution today. Consider what makes them distinct from the general market and why they would be willing to try something new.

### 3. Articulate the Unique Value Proposition
Write a single, clear sentence that explains why your solution is different and worth paying attention to. This should communicate the primary benefit in language your customers would use, not industry jargon. Test it by asking: would a stranger understand what you offer and why it matters within five seconds?

### 4. Outline the Solution
For each problem identified in Step 1, describe the simplest possible feature or capability that addresses it. Resist the urge to over-engineer; focus on the minimum viable solution that would make early adopters switch from their current alternatives. Map each solution element directly to a specific problem.

### 5. Map Channels
Identify how you will reach your customer segments. List both paid and organic channels, and rank them by cost-effectiveness and scalability. Consider where your early adopters already spend their time and how they discover new products in your category.

### 6. Define Revenue Streams
Specify how you will make money. Define the pricing model (subscription, one-time, freemium, usage-based), the price point, and the rationale behind it. Estimate the customer lifetime value and how many customers you need to reach your revenue targets.

### 7. Estimate Cost Structure
List your fixed and variable costs, including development, marketing, operations, and support. Identify which costs are essential for launch versus those that can be deferred. Calculate your burn rate and how long your runway extends before you need revenue or funding.

### 8. Identify Key Metrics
Define 3-5 metrics that will tell you whether your business model is working. These should cover acquisition, activation, retention, revenue, and referral. For each metric, set a baseline target for your first 90 days and explain how you will measure it.

### 9. Articulate Unfair Advantage
Describe what you have that cannot be easily copied or bought by competitors. This could be domain expertise, proprietary data, existing relationships, network effects, or a unique team composition. Be honest if you do not have one yet and outline how you plan to build one over time.

## Expected Output

A completed Lean Canvas document with all nine blocks filled in, a ranked list of the riskiest assumptions, and a 30-day validation plan with specific experiments to test each assumption.

## Recommended Agents

- **CEO** — Drives the overall vision and ensures the canvas aligns with long-term strategic goals.
- **Product Manager** — Structures the problem-solution fit and prioritizes which assumptions to validate first.
- **Market Researcher** — Provides data on customer segments, competitors, and market sizing to ground the canvas in evidence.
- **CFO** — Validates the revenue model, cost structure, and financial projections for feasibility.
