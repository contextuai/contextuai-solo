# Video 9: Connections — Social Publishing

> **Director's Context:** Connections in ContextuAI Solo integrate external platforms — Telegram, Discord, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, Facebook. These allow Crews to publish content directly to social channels. There are two connection methods: token-paste (Telegram, Discord, Twitter) and OAuth2 (LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook). Connections are NOT personas — this is a common confusion. Connections are about publishing and external communication. Personas are about chat behavior.

**Duration:** 3 minutes
**Goal:** Show how to connect platforms and use them with Crews for social publishing.

---

## Opening (0:00 - 0:10)

**On screen:** Connections page showing 6 platform cards with status indicators

**Voiceover:**
> "Connections link Solo to the outside world — publish content from your AI crews directly to social platforms."

## Scene 1: Available Platforms (0:10 - 0:35)

**On screen:** Highlight each platform card — show the 6 options

**Voiceover:**
> "Six platforms out of the box. Telegram and Discord use token-paste — you create a bot, copy the token, paste it here. Twitter/X uses an API key. LinkedIn, Instagram, and Facebook use OAuth — click connect, authorize in your browser, done. Each card shows you a step-by-step setup guide."

**Key point for NotebookLM:** Keep the setup explanation simple. Users don't need to understand OAuth vs tokens — they just need to know which button to click. The app guides them through platform-specific setup.

---

## Scene 2: Connecting LinkedIn (0:35 - 1:10)

**On screen:** Click LinkedIn card → OAuth flow → browser popup → authorize → connected badge

**Voiceover:**
> "Let's connect LinkedIn. Click the LinkedIn card. Solo opens your browser to LinkedIn's authorization page. Log in, click 'Allow', and you're redirected back. The card now shows 'Connected' with a green badge. That's it — Solo can now publish to your LinkedIn profile."

---

## Scene 3: Connecting Telegram (1:10 - 1:40)

**On screen:** Click Telegram card → show BotFather instructions → paste token → connected

**Voiceover:**
> "For Telegram, you need a bot token. Solo shows you exactly how — open Telegram, message BotFather, type '/newbot', follow the prompts, and copy the token. Paste it into Solo and you're connected. Your Telegram bot can now receive and send messages through your Crews."

---

## Scene 4: Using Connections in Crews (1:40 - 2:20)

**On screen:** Crew Builder Step 4 → toggle LinkedIn and Twitter → set approval required

**Voiceover:**
> "Here's where it comes together. When building a Crew, Step 4 lets you toggle which connections to use. Enable LinkedIn and Twitter for your content crew. Set 'Approval Required' on — the crew drafts the post, but it won't publish until you review and approve. You get a preview of exactly what will be posted to each platform."

**Key point for NotebookLM:** The approval workflow is the key message here. AI-generated content going to social media needs human review. Solo makes this a first-class feature, not an afterthought.

---

## Scene 5: Inbound Connections (2:20 - 2:45)

**On screen:** Show a Telegram message coming in → crew processes it → response sent back

**Voiceover:**
> "Connections work both ways. Telegram and Discord can receive messages too. Set up a support bot crew and incoming messages get processed by your agents — FAQ responses, triage, escalation. With approval turned on, you review every outgoing reply before it's sent."
