# Video 7: Crews — Multi-Agent Teams

**Duration:** 4 minutes
**Goal:** Walk through the full crew creation wizard and show a crew execution with real-time progress.

---

## Opening (0:00 - 0:15)

**On screen:** Crews page with stat cards

**Voiceover:**
> "Crews are the most powerful feature in Solo. Instead of asking one AI for help, you assemble a team of specialists that work together — researching, writing, reviewing, and delivering. Let's build one."

---

## Scene 1: The Crew Dashboard (0:15 - 0:40)

**On screen:** Crews main page

**Steps:**
1. Show stat cards: Total Crews, Running, Completed, Failed
2. Show the Crews tab with existing crew cards
3. Show the Runs tab with execution history
4. Point out search and filters (status, execution mode)

**Voiceover:**
> "The crew dashboard shows your teams and their track record. You can see how many are running, completed, or failed. The Runs tab gives you full execution history. Let's create a new crew."

---

## Scene 2: Step 1 — Crew Details (0:40 - 1:10)

**On screen:** Click "Create Crew" → Step 1

**Steps:**
1. Click **Create Crew** — wizard opens
2. Enter name: "Weekly Blog Pipeline"
3. Enter description or click **"Use Blueprint"** → select Content blueprint
4. Show model selector — leave on "Auto" or pick a specific model

**Voiceover:**
> "Give your crew a name and description. Pro tip — use a blueprint to pre-fill a proven workflow. You can also pick a specific AI model, or leave it on Auto to use the default."

---

## Scene 3: Step 2 — Execution Mode (1:10 - 1:40)

**On screen:** Step 2 — four execution mode cards

**Steps:**
1. Show all four mode cards with descriptions
2. Click **Sequential** — highlight: "Agents run one after another, output passes forward"
3. Briefly show **Autonomous** mode — point out the safety limits (max invocations, budget cap)

**Voiceover:**
> "Choose how your agents collaborate. Sequential is the most common — Agent A's output feeds into Agent B. Parallel runs everyone at once. Pipeline handles branching dependencies. And Autonomous lets a coordinator discover and invoke agents dynamically — with safety limits you control."

---

## Scene 4: Step 3 — Agent Team (1:40 - 2:20)

**On screen:** Step 3 — agent list

**Steps:**
1. Click **Browse Library** — show the library panel
2. Search "content" → click **Content Strategist** to add
3. Search "copy" → click **Copywriter** to add
4. Add a third: **SEO Specialist**
5. Show the agent pipeline visualization (three agents with arrows)
6. Point out each agent's name, role, and instructions fields

**Voiceover:**
> "Build your team from the 81-agent library. Browse, search, and click to add. I'm building a content pipeline — Content Strategist plans the topic, Copywriter drafts it, SEO Specialist optimizes it. You can see the pipeline visualization showing the flow."

---

## Scene 5: Step 4 — Connections (2:20 - 2:45)

**On screen:** Step 4 — channel cards

**Steps:**
1. Show the 6 channel cards (Telegram, Discord, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, Facebook)
2. Select **LinkedIn** and **Twitter/X**
3. Check "Require approval before sending" for both
4. Show the connection summary with approval badges

**Voiceover:**
> "Optionally connect your crew to social channels. I'll add LinkedIn and Twitter with approval required — the crew will draft posts but wait for my sign-off before publishing. This step is optional if your crew doesn't need social publishing."

---

## Scene 6: Step 5 — Review & Run (2:45 - 3:30)

**On screen:** Step 5 review → Create → Run

**Steps:**
1. Show the review summary: crew name, mode, model, agents, connections
2. Click **Create Crew**
3. Click **Run** on the new crew card
4. Show the progress modal:
   - Status: Running
   - Progress bar advancing
   - Step timeline: Content Strategist (completed) → Copywriter (running) → SEO Specialist (pending)
   - Duration and token counters ticking
5. Show final "Completed" status with output

**Voiceover:**
> "Review everything, then create. Now hit Run. The progress modal shows real-time status — each agent's step with duration and tokens. Watch the pipeline: Content Strategist finishes, hands off to the Copywriter, who passes to SEO. When it's done, you get the final compiled output."

---

## Closing (3:30 - 4:00)

**On screen:** Crew card with "Completed" badge and the Runs tab

**Voiceover:**
> "That's a crew. Define it once, run it whenever you need it. Check the Runs tab for history, costs, and outputs. You just built a content pipeline that would take a human team hours — and it ran in minutes. Next, let's look at Workspace for deeper project work."

**End card:** "Next: Workspace — Run Projects"
