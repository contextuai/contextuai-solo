# Video 9: Connections — Social Publishing

**Duration:** 3 minutes
**Goal:** Show how to connect external platforms and use them with crews.

---

## Opening (0:00 - 0:10)

**Voiceover:**
> "Connections let your AI crews publish to the real world — Telegram, Discord, LinkedIn, Twitter, Instagram, and Facebook. Let's set them up."

---

## Scene 1: The 6 Platforms (0:10 - 0:35)

**On screen:** Connections page with all 6 cards

**Steps:**
1. Show all 6 connection cards: Telegram, Discord, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, Facebook
2. Point out the Connected/Disconnected badges
3. Explain two types:
   - **Token-paste:** Telegram, Discord, Twitter/X — paste a bot token or API key
   - **OAuth:** LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook — sign in with your account

**Voiceover:**
> "Six platforms, two connection methods. Telegram, Discord, and Twitter use token-paste — you create a bot and paste the credentials. LinkedIn, Instagram, and Facebook use OAuth — sign in directly. Let's connect a couple."

---

## Scene 2: Connecting Telegram (0:35 - 1:10)

**On screen:** Expand Telegram card

**Steps:**
1. Click the Telegram card to expand
2. Show the setup instructions: "Create a bot via @BotFather"
3. Paste a bot token
4. Click **Save**
5. Show the "Connected" badge appearing
6. Show the direction toggles: Inbound + Outbound both enabled

**Voiceover:**
> "Telegram is the easiest — create a bot with BotFather in about 30 seconds, paste the token, save. Now it's connected. Both inbound and outbound are enabled, meaning your crew can receive messages from users and send responses."

---

## Scene 3: Connecting LinkedIn (OAuth) (1:10 - 1:50)

**On screen:** Expand LinkedIn card

**Steps:**
1. Click LinkedIn card to expand
2. Show the setup instructions with "How to connect LinkedIn" help text
3. Enter Client ID and Client Secret
4. Show the callback URL that needs to be added to the LinkedIn app
5. Click **"Sign in with LinkedIn"**
6. Show the OAuth flow completing
7. Show "Connected" badge with profile name

**Voiceover:**
> "LinkedIn uses OAuth. Enter your app credentials from the LinkedIn Developer Portal, add the callback URL to your app settings, and sign in. Once connected, you'll see your profile name and a 'Connected' badge. LinkedIn is outbound only — your crews can post, but don't receive messages."

---

## Scene 4: Using Connections in Crews (1:50 - 2:35)

**On screen:** Crew Builder → Step 4

**Steps:**
1. Go to Crews → Create or Edit a crew
2. Navigate to Step 4 (Connections)
3. Show the connected channels with checkmarks
4. Select Telegram and LinkedIn
5. Check **"Require approval"** on LinkedIn
6. Show the connection summary: Telegram (no approval), LinkedIn (approval required)
7. Quick flash of the approval flow — crew pauses, shows draft, user approves

**Voiceover:**
> "When creating a crew, Step 4 lets you bind it to your connected channels. Select which platforms this crew publishes to. I'm enabling approval on LinkedIn — the crew will draft the post and wait for my review before publishing. Telegram will auto-send. This gives you control where it matters."

---

## Closing (2:35 - 3:00)

**On screen:** Connections page with multiple "Connected" badges

**Voiceover:**
> "Set up your connections once, use them across any crew. Your AI team drafts, you approve, it publishes. Next up — Settings, Brand Voice, and making Solo truly yours."

**End card:** "Next: Settings & Brand Voice"
