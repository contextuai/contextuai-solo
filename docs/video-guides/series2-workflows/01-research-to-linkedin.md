# Workflow 1: Solo Consultant — Research to LinkedIn Post

**Duration:** 6 minutes
**Scenario:** A freelance consultant researches a market trend, produces a thought-leadership article, and publishes it to LinkedIn — all from Solo.
**Flow:** Workspace (research) → Crew (write + review) → LinkedIn (publish)

---

## Opening (0:00 - 0:20)

**On screen:** Split screen — Solo app on left, LinkedIn profile on right

**Voiceover:**
> "You're a solo consultant. You want to publish a thought-leadership post on LinkedIn about AI adoption in mid-market companies. Normally this takes half a day — research, draft, edit, post. With Solo, we'll do it in minutes by chaining Workspace, Crews, and Connections together."

---

## Act 1: Research in Workspace (0:20 - 2:00)

**On screen:** Workspace → New Project

**Steps:**
1. Click **New Project**
2. Name: "AI Adoption in Mid-Market B2B — Research Brief"
3. Description: "Research current AI adoption trends in mid-market B2B companies (50-500 employees). Focus on adoption rates, top use cases, barriers to adoption, and ROI data from recent surveys and reports."
4. Click **"Use Blueprint"** → select "Market Research" blueprint
5. Select agents:
   - **Competitive Intelligence Analyst** — gathers market data
   - **Data Analyst** — structures findings
6. Click **Create** → **Execute**
7. Show execution running
8. Show completed results:
   - **Discussion tab:** Analyst's raw research, Data Analyst's structured findings
   - **Compiled Output tab:** Clean research brief with stats, trends, and sources

**Voiceover:**
> "First, I create a Workspace project for the research phase. I'm using the Market Research blueprint and assigning two agents — a Competitive Intelligence Analyst to gather data and a Data Analyst to structure it. Let me run this... The Discussion tab shows each agent's work. The Compiled Output gives me a clean research brief with real data points I can reference."

**Key moment:** Copy the compiled output to clipboard (click copy button)

---

## Act 2: Write & Review with a Crew (2:00 - 4:00)

**On screen:** Crews → Create Crew

**Steps:**
1. Click **Create Crew**
2. Name: "LinkedIn Thought Leadership Writer"
3. Description: Paste the research brief from workspace, plus: "Using this research, write a LinkedIn thought-leadership post (800-1200 words) about AI adoption in mid-market B2B. Target audience: VPs of Operations and CROs."
4. Execution mode: **Sequential**
5. Agents from library:
   - **Content Strategist** — structures the narrative arc
   - **Copywriter** — writes the post in brand voice
   - **SEO Specialist** — optimizes for LinkedIn algorithm (hashtags, formatting)
6. Connections: Select **LinkedIn** with **approval required**
7. Review and **Create**
8. Click **Run**
9. Show progress:
   - Content Strategist: outlines the post structure (completed)
   - Copywriter: drafts the full post (running → completed)
   - SEO Specialist: adds hashtags, formatting tweaks (running → completed)
10. Show the final output — a polished LinkedIn post

**Voiceover:**
> "Now I create a crew to write the post. I paste my research brief as context and add three agents in sequence — Content Strategist plans the structure, Copywriter drafts it, SEO Specialist polishes for LinkedIn. I've connected LinkedIn with approval required. Let me run it... Watch the pipeline: strategy, then draft, then optimize. The final output is a publication-ready LinkedIn post with hashtags and formatting."

---

## Act 3: Approve & Publish (4:00 - 5:00)

**On screen:** Approval flow

**Steps:**
1. Show the crew pausing for approval before publishing to LinkedIn
2. Review the drafted post
3. Make a small edit (optional — show that you can tweak before approving)
4. Click **Approve**
5. Show the post published to LinkedIn

**Voiceover:**
> "Because I enabled approval, the crew pauses before posting. I can review the draft, make a quick tweak if needed, and approve. The post goes live on LinkedIn. From research to published post — without leaving Solo."

---

**On screen:** Flow diagram:
