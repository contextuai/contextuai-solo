# Workflow 7: Competitive Intel to Board Deck

**Duration:** 7 minutes
**Scenario:** A startup founder prepares for a board meeting by gathering competitive intelligence, producing a strategic analysis, and refining the narrative in Chat.
**Flow:** Workspace (research) → Crew (synthesis + deck narrative) → Chat (Q&A prep)

---

## Opening (0:00 - 0:20)

**Voiceover:**
> "Board meeting in 3 days. You need a competitive landscape update, strategic positioning analysis, and a narrative that makes investors confident. Let's build the full deck story with Solo."

---

## Act 1: Competitive Research in Workspace (0:20 - 2:00)

**On screen:** Workspace → New Project

**Steps:**
1. New Project: "Competitive Landscape — Q1 Board Update"
2. Description: "Research and analyze our top 5 competitors in the project management SaaS space. For each competitor: recent product launches, pricing changes, funding rounds, key hires, and market positioning shifts. Then identify: emerging threats, market gaps we can exploit, and our relative strengths."
3. Click **"Use Blueprint"** → select "Competitive Analysis"
4. Agents:
   - **Competitive Intelligence Analyst** — gathers intel on all 5 competitors
   - **Data Analyst** — structures findings into comparable metrics
   - **Trend Analyst** — identifies market-level patterns
5. Execute
6. Show results:
   - Discussion tab: Each agent's detailed research
   - Compiled Output: structured competitive matrix with company profiles, strengths/weaknesses, market trends

**Voiceover:**
> "Workspace handles the research phase. Three analysts dig into five competitors — product changes, pricing, funding, hiring. The compiled output is a competitive matrix with structured profiles and market trends. This would take a junior analyst a week of Googling."

---

## Act 2: Strategic Synthesis Crew (2:00 - 4:00)

**On screen:** Crews → Create Crew

**Steps:**
1. Crew: "Board Deck Strategy Writer"
2. Paste the competitive analysis
3. Description: "Transform this competitive analysis into a board-ready strategic narrative. Structure:
   - Slide 1: Market landscape summary (one-paragraph overview)
   - Slide 2: Competitive positioning map (describe our position vs 5 competitors across 2 axes)
   - Slide 3: Threats & opportunities (top 3 of each, with evidence)
   - Slide 4: Strategic recommendations (3 actionable moves for next quarter)
   - Slide 5: Competitive moat assessment (what's defensible, what's not)
   - Speaker notes for each slide
   Tone: confident, data-backed, forward-looking."
4. Mode: **Sequential**
5. Agents:
   - **CEO Strategic Advisor** — strategic framing and board-level narrative
   - **Product Manager** — product positioning recommendations
   - **Copywriter** — polishes language and ensures clarity
6. Create → Run
7. Show progress through all three agents
8. Output: 5-slide narrative with speaker notes

**Voiceover:**
> "The crew transforms raw research into a board narrative. CEO Strategic Advisor frames the story investors want to hear — confident but honest. Product Manager adds positioning depth. Copywriter ensures every sentence lands. Five slides with speaker notes, all in board-ready language."

---

## Act 3: Q&A Prep in Chat (4:00 - 5:45)

**On screen:** Chat page

**Steps:**
1. New chat session — paste the deck narrative
2. Ask: "You're a skeptical board member. What are the 5 hardest questions you'd ask about this competitive analysis?"
3. Show 5 tough questions (e.g., "Why aren't you concerned about Competitor X's Series C?", "Your moat analysis seems optimistic — what happens if they copy Feature Y?")
4. For each question, ask: "Draft a confident but honest answer for question 3"
5. Show a well-structured answer
6. Ask: "What data point am I missing that would make this presentation bulletproof?"
7. Show response suggesting missing metrics

**Voiceover:**
> "Here's the secret weapon — Chat for board prep. I paste the deck and ask the AI to play a skeptical board member. It generates the hard questions investors will actually ask. Then I draft answers for each one. Finally, I ask what data I'm missing. Now I'm walking into that board meeting prepared for anything."

---

## Act 4: Final Polish (5:45 - 6:30)

**On screen:** Chat continuation

**Steps:**
1. Ask: "Rewrite Slide 1 (market landscape) to open with our strongest data point"
2. Show revised opening
3. Ask: "Make the strategic recommendations more specific — include timeline and owner for each"
4. Show revised recommendations with Q1 milestones

**Voiceover:**
> "Last refinements in Chat. I sharpen the opening slide to lead with our best number. I make the recommendations more actionable with timelines. This iterative polish is what separates a good deck from a great one."

---
