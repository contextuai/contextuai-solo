# Workflow 4: Product Launch in a Day

**Duration:** 8 minutes
**Scenario:** A solo founder launches a new feature with strategy, landing page copy, email campaign, social posts — all produced and distributed from Solo in one day.
**Flow:** Blueprint → Workspace (strategy) → Crew 1 (content) → Crew 2 (social distribution) → Multi-channel publish

---

## Opening (0:00 - 0:20)

**Voiceover:**
> "You're launching a new feature next week. You need a positioning strategy, landing page copy, an email announcement, and social posts across three platforms. With a marketing team, this takes a week. Solo can do it in a day."

---

## Act 1: Strategy in Workspace (0:20 - 2:00)

**On screen:** Workspace → New Project

**Steps:**
1. New Project: "Feature Launch Strategy — Smart Dashboard Analytics"
2. Click **"Use Blueprint"** → select "Product Launch Strategy" blueprint
3. Description: "We're launching 'Smart Dashboard Analytics' — an AI-powered analytics feature in our project management tool. Target: VP of Operations at mid-market companies. Key differentiator: automated insight generation that saves 5 hours/week of manual reporting."
4. Agents:
   - **Product Manager** — positioning and messaging framework
   - **Competitive Intelligence Analyst** — competitive landscape
   - **CMO Strategic Advisor** — go-to-market recommendations
5. Execute → Show results
6. Compiled output: positioning statement, key messages, competitive advantages, launch timeline

**Voiceover:**
> "Start with strategy. I use the Product Launch blueprint, describe the feature, and assign three strategists. The Product Manager defines positioning, the Competitive Analyst maps the landscape, and the CMO frames the go-to-market plan. The output is a complete launch strategy I can build on."

**Key moment:** Copy the strategy output

---

## Act 2: Content Production Crew (2:00 - 4:00)

**On screen:** Crews → Create Crew

**Steps:**
1. Crew: "Launch Content Producer"
2. Paste strategy as context
3. Add specific deliverables: "Produce: 1) Landing page hero section + 3 benefit blocks, 2) Product announcement email (subject line + body), 3) Press release draft"
4. Mode: **Sequential**
5. Agents:
   - **Content Strategist** — content plan aligned to strategy
   - **Copywriter** — writes all three pieces
   - **SEO Specialist** — optimizes landing page copy for search
   - **Email Marketing Specialist** — refines the email for conversion
6. No connections (content production, not publishing)
7. Create → Run
8. Show all four agents completing in sequence
9. Show output: landing page copy, email, press release

**Voiceover:**
> "Now the content crew. I feed in the strategy and assign four agents: Content Strategist plans what to write, Copywriter produces all three pieces, SEO optimizes the landing page, and Email Marketing refines the announcement email. Sequential — each one improves what came before. Output: landing page copy, product email, and a press release draft."

---

## Act 3: Social Distribution Crew (4:00 - 5:45)

**On screen:** Create a second crew

**Steps:**
1. Crew: "Launch Social Distribution"
2. Paste the content crew's output
3. Description: "Adapt this launch content for social media. Create: 1) LinkedIn announcement post (professional, data-driven), 2) Twitter/X thread (5 tweets, punchy and visual), 3) Instagram caption (exciting, visual-first)"
4. Mode: **Sequential**
5. Agents:
   - **Social Media Manager** — adapts content per platform
   - **Brand Voice Guardian** — ensures consistency
6. Connections: LinkedIn + Twitter/X + Instagram, all with approval
7. Create → Run
8. Show agents completing
9. Approval flow:
   - LinkedIn post → Review → Approve
   - Twitter thread → Review → Approve
   - Instagram caption → Review → Approve

**Voiceover:**
> "Second crew: Social Distribution. I paste the content and have the Social Media Manager adapt it for each platform, with the Brand Voice Guardian checking consistency. All three channels connected with approval. Run it, review each piece, approve. Three platforms, one click each."

---

## Act 4: Review Everything in Chat (5:45 - 6:45)

**On screen:** Chat session

**Steps:**
1. New chat — paste the landing page copy
2. Ask: "Review this landing page copy. Is the value proposition clear in the first 5 seconds? Suggest improvements."
3. Show response with specific improvements
4. Ask: "Write 3 alternative headline options that lead with the '5 hours saved' benefit"
5. Pick the best one

**Voiceover:**
> "Final review in Chat. I paste the landing page copy and ask for a cold-eye review. The AI spots where the value prop could be sharper and suggests three alternative headlines. I pick the strongest one. This is where your judgment makes the difference."

---
