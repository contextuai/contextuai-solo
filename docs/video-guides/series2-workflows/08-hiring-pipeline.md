# Workflow 8: Hiring Pipeline — Job Post to Outreach

**Duration:** 6 minutes
**Scenario:** A startup founder creates a job description, generates outreach content, and publishes it across LinkedIn, Twitter, and a Telegram recruiting channel.
**Flow:** Workspace (JD + requirements) → Crew (content adaptation) → Multi-channel publish (LinkedIn + Twitter + Telegram)

---

## Opening (0:00 - 0:15)

**Voiceover:**
> "You're hiring your first senior engineer. You need a compelling job description, LinkedIn outreach, a Twitter announcement, and a post in your Telegram community — all consistent, all on-brand. Solo's HR and marketing agents can handle the entire pipeline."

---

## Act 1: Job Description in Workspace (0:15 - 1:45)

**On screen:** Workspace → New Project

**Steps:**
1. New Project: "Senior Frontend Engineer — Job Description"
2. Description: "Create a comprehensive job description for a Senior Frontend Engineer. Company: a 15-person B2B SaaS startup building project management tools. Tech stack: React 19, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, Vite, Tauri. The role includes: building the core product UI, contributing to design system, mentoring a junior developer, and working directly with the CTO. Compensation: $150K-$180K + equity. Remote-first, US time zones."
3. Agents:
   - **Talent Acquisition Specialist** — structures the JD with market-standard sections
   - **Copywriter** — makes it compelling and authentic (not corporate boilerplate)
   - **People Operations Manager** — ensures compliance and inclusivity
4. Execute
5. Show results:
   - Discussion: Each agent's contribution
   - Compiled Output: polished JD with sections: About Us, The Role, What You'll Do, Requirements, Nice-to-Haves, Benefits, How to Apply

**Voiceover:**
> "Workspace produces the core JD. Talent Acquisition structures it properly, Copywriter makes it read like a real human wrote it — not corporate HR-speak, and People Operations ensures inclusive language and compliance. The output is a job description that top engineers would actually read."

**Key moment:** Copy compiled output

---

## Act 2: Content Adaptation Crew (1:45 - 3:30)

**On screen:** Crews → Create Crew

**Steps:**
1. Crew: "Hiring Content Pipeline"
2. Paste the JD
3. Description: "Using this job description, create:
   1. **LinkedIn job post** — professional but authentic, 300-400 words, includes key highlights and a direct apply link. Written from the CTO's perspective.
   2. **Twitter/X thread** — 4-5 tweets. Hook with the role + one compelling detail. Share what makes the team special. End with how to apply. Casual, engineer-friendly tone.
   3. **Telegram community post** — short, direct, community-style. 150 words max. Focus on the technical challenge and team culture. Include a link to the full JD.
   Each piece should feel native to its platform."
4. Mode: **Sequential**
5. Agents:
   - **Content Strategist** — plans the adaptation strategy per platform
   - **Social Media Manager** — writes platform-native content
   - **Brand Voice Guardian** — ensures consistent employer brand
6. Connections:
   - LinkedIn (approval required)
   - Twitter/X (approval required)
   - Telegram (approval required)
7. Create → Run
8. Show progress:
   - Content Strategist: platform adaptation plan (completed)
   - Social Media Manager: all three pieces (completed)
   - Brand Voice Guardian: consistency check (completed)
9. Show output: three distinct pieces, each native to its platform

**Voiceover:**
> "The crew adapts the JD for three platforms. Content Strategist plans the angle for each — LinkedIn gets the CTO's perspective, Twitter gets the punchy thread, Telegram gets the community vibe. Social Media Manager writes all three. Brand Voice Guardian checks that our employer brand is consistent. Three pieces, three voices, one message."

---

## Act 3: Review & Publish (3:30 - 5:00)

**On screen:** Approval flow

**LinkedIn Post:**
1. Show the drafted LinkedIn post — CTO perspective, professional but human
2. Quick edit: tweak one line to add a personal detail
3. Approve → published

**Twitter Thread:**
1. Show the 4-tweet thread — hook, team culture, tech stack, CTA
2. Approve → published

**Telegram Post:**
1. Show the community post — short, direct, technical
2. Approve → published

**Voiceover:**
> "Review time. The LinkedIn post reads like the CTO actually wrote it — I'll add one personal touch and approve. The Twitter thread hooks with the role and closes with how to apply — approve. Telegram post is direct and community-native — approve. All three go live within seconds of each other."

---

## Act 4: Bonus — Outreach Templates in Chat (5:00 - 5:40)

**On screen:** Chat page

**Steps:**
1. New chat — paste the JD
2. Ask: "Write 3 personalized outreach templates for reaching out to potential candidates on LinkedIn. Tone: respectful, not pushy. Include a placeholder for their specific work I admire."
3. Show three outreach templates, each with a different angle:
   - Template 1: Technical challenge focused
   - Template 2: Team and culture focused
   - Template 3: Growth opportunity focused

**Voiceover:**
> "Bonus: I use Chat to generate outreach templates for direct recruiting. Three angles — technical challenge, team culture, career growth. Each has a placeholder where I can reference the candidate's specific work. Personal outreach that doesn't feel like spam."
