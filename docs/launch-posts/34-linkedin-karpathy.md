**Target:** Comment on Karpathy's LinkedIn posts about AI education, local models, or making AI accessible

---

Your work on making AI understandable inspired the design philosophy behind ContextuAI Solo.

The biggest barrier to local AI isn't the models — it's the UX. Terminal commands, config files, Python environments, CUDA drivers. Non-technical business professionals shouldn't need to learn any of that.

So I built a desktop app where you open it, click "Download" next to a model, and start chatting. 35+ GGUF models — DeepSeek R1, Qwen 3.5, Llama 3.1, Phi-4, Mistral — running on CPU. No GPU, no terminal, no setup.

Added 93+ pre-built business agents on top so users don't need to figure out prompting either. Pick "Financial Analyst" or "Content Strategist" and it just works.

Making AI accessible means removing every friction point, not just the technical ones.

GitHub: https://github.com/contextuai/contextuai-solo
