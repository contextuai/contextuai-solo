# Blueprints

Blueprints are workflow templates that give your crews and workspace projects a head start. Instead of writing descriptions and instructions from scratch, pick a blueprint and customize it for your needs.

![Blueprints](../screenshots/009-Blueprints.png)

---

## Getting Started

1. Navigate to **Blueprints** from the sidebar.
2. Browse the library of pre-built templates.
3. Use blueprints when creating Crews (Step 1) or Workspace projects.

## Pre-Built Blueprints

ContextuAI Solo ships with 10 blueprints across 5 categories:

| Category | What it covers |
|----------|---------------|
| **Strategy** | Business planning, competitive analysis, market entry |
| **Content** | Content calendars, blog pipelines, editorial workflows |
| **Marketing** | Campaign planning, SEO audits, social media strategies |
| **Product** | Feature prioritization, user research, roadmap planning |
| **Research** | Market research, trend analysis, competitive intelligence |

## Blueprint Cards

Each blueprint card shows:

- **Category badge** (color-coded)
- **Name and description**
- **Tags** (up to 4 displayed)
- **Source** — "Library" for built-in or "Custom" for user-created
- **Usage count** — how many times it's been used

## Searching and Filtering

- **Search** — filter blueprints by name in real-time
- **Category filter** — narrow down by Strategy, Product, Marketing, Content, Research, Operations, or General
- **Source filter** — show only Library (built-in) or Custom (user-created) blueprints
- All filters work together

## Creating a Custom Blueprint

![Create Blueprint](../screenshots/010-CreateBlueprint.png)

1. Click **Create Blueprint**.
2. Fill in the form:
   - **Name** (required) — a descriptive title
   - **Description** — what this workflow accomplishes
   - **Category** — select from the dropdown
   - **Tags** — comma-separated keywords (converted to lowercase)
   - **Content** (required) — the workflow template in Markdown format
3. Click **Create**.

### Writing Blueprint Content

Blueprint content is written in Markdown. A good blueprint typically includes:

- **Objective** — what the workflow achieves
- **Steps** — numbered sequence of tasks
- **Agent roles** — which types of agents should handle each step
- **Expected output** — what the final deliverable looks like

Example:
```markdown
## Objective
Create a comprehensive competitive analysis report.

## Steps
1. **Research** — Identify top 5 competitors and gather public data
2. **Analyze** — Compare pricing, features, market positioning
3. **Synthesize** — Write executive summary with strategic recommendations

## Expected Output
A structured report with competitor profiles, SWOT analysis, and action items.
```

## Using Blueprints

Blueprints can be used in two places:

### In Crew Builder (Step 1)
When creating a crew, click **"Use Blueprint"** to select a template. The blueprint content populates the crew description.

### In Workspace Projects
When creating a new workspace project, click **"Use Blueprint"** to start from a template instead of a blank slate.

## Managing Blueprints

- **Preview** — hover over a card and click the preview icon to read the full content
- **Delete** — hover and click the trash icon (custom blueprints only; built-in blueprints cannot be deleted)
- **Refresh** — click the refresh button to reload the list

## Tips

- **Start with a built-in blueprint** to understand the format before creating your own.
- **Blueprints are starting points** — always customize them for your specific business context after applying.
- **Create blueprints for recurring workflows** — if you find yourself writing the same crew description repeatedly, save it as a blueprint.
- **Use tags** to make your custom blueprints easier to find later.
